Bring Your Own Issue


Editor's Note

As we discussed ideas for our 2019 Spring Issue, only one theme tasted right. We wanted to explore what food and consumption mean to our readers, hoping to make a space for both it’s sumptuousness and its fractures. For women, food is often unrecognized labor and a fierce, loving celebration of everything they are. For immigrants, food is home. We could go on—food is a riotous art, a long look at your belly in the mirror, a joyous, miraculous testament to being alive. We hungered for this issue, which is a community effort in its very essence, its pages a potluck—Bring your Own (BYO). These words and pictures are yours.

Utopia-Healing Issue


Editor's Note

For our Utopia-Healing Issue, Blacklight asked its contributors to dream up radical new languages for whole new worlds— our utopias as we imagine them; futuristic, free, tethered to our roots, sparkling stars. Artists have always turned to speculation, to science fiction, to invention, in order to reimagine what the world could look like when it has been emptied of its cruelty, of its injustice, of the systems of oppression that have been buried into its soil. Through creation, we can make a place for ourselves in the world, even if it does not yet exist. This issue is a little slice of those utopias, a journey into that deep and ancient healing, wherever it may be— a rainy afternoon, making community with our people, eating food at a wedding, full-throated revolution, in a different galaxy.
