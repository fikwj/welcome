About

Fikayo is a researcher, paralegal, and occasional multimedia producer working within the digital rights, speech, and privacy space. She is interested in exploring themes of surveillance, kinship, digital culture, urban planning, transnationalism and affective communities. Fikayo received her bachelors from the University of Chicago with a dual degree in Sociology & Public Policy and a specialization in Gender & Sexuality.

Once upon a time, she co-hosted and produced a bi-weekly radio show on WHPK 88.5 FM called OurSpace. The show explored technology and internet culture at large, and how the intersections of gender, race, class, and sexuality inform the way we engage with it.

She resonates deeply with this sentence from Octavia Butler's author bio.

She is open to part-time freelance and volunteer opportunities as a research associate or audio production assistant.
